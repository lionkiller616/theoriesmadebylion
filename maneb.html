4. Theoretical Operation & Energy Flow

The system operates in a continuous loop, where quantum harvesting provides primary energy, the battery stack stores and delivers power, the biofilm matrix recycles key ions, and a sophisticated controller optimizes pathways and responses.

Energy Flow Equation

The net change in stored energy \(E\) over time \(t\) can be described as:

$$\frac{dE}{dt} = P_{harvest} - P_{load} - P_{loss}$$

Where:

  • \(P_{harvest}\) is the total power from AQRC (ZPE + RF).
  • \(P_{load}\) is the power delivered to the external load.
  • \(P_{loss} = I^2R_{int} + P_{biofilm\_maint} + P_{thermal}\) represents internal losses, including:
    • Ohmic losses (\(I^2R_{int}\)), with internal resistance \(R_{int} \approx 0.12 \text{ m}\Omega\).
    • Energy for biofilm maintenance (\(P_{biofilm\_maint}\)).
    • Thermal dissipation losses (\(P_{thermal}\)).

More detailed electrochemical representation:

$$\frac{dE}{dt} = (P_{ZPE} + P_{RF}) - I \cdot V_{load} - I^2R_{int} - \frac{dQ_{rxn}}{dt}\Delta\phi_{cell}$$

Where \(\frac{dQ_{rxn}}{dt}\Delta\phi_{cell}\) accounts for electrochemical reaction energies and overpotentials.
