High-Entropy Alloy Superconductors

Combinatorial Materials Screening for New $T_c$ Peaks

grain1. Introduction

High-entropy alloys (HEAs) are multi-principal-element materials—typically containing five or more elements in near-equimolar ratios—that exhibit high configurational entropy, leading to stabilized solid solutions with unique mechanical and electronic properties. Recent discoveries of superconductivity in HEAs (e.g., Ta–Nb–Hf–Zr–Ti) motivate combinatorial screening to identify compositions with enhanced critical temperatures ($T_c$).

Goals:

  • Explore compositional space of multi-component alloys
  • Predict and validate $T_c$ peaks via high-throughput methods
  • Uncover mechanisms linking entropy, electronic structure, and superconductivity

layers2. High-Entropy Alloy Fundamentals

  • Configurational Entropy: $\Delta S_{conf} = -R \sum_i x_i \ln x_i$ for mole fractions $x_i$
  • Phase Stability: High $\Delta S_{conf}$ favors simple solid-solution phases (bcc, fcc)
  • Valence Electron Concentration (VEC): Key parameter influencing electronic DOS near Fermi level

science3. Superconductivity Theory

3.1 BCS Framework

Critical temperature approximation (McMillan formula):

$$ T_c = \frac{\Theta_D}{1.45} \exp\Bigl(-\frac{1.04(1+\lambda)}{\lambda - \mu^*(1+0.62\lambda)}\Bigr) $$
  • $\Theta_D$: Debye temperature
  • $\lambda$: electron–phonon coupling constant
  • $\mu^*$: Coulomb pseudopotential (~0.1)

3.2 Electronic Density of States

  • DOS at Fermi level $N(0)$ correlates with $\lambda \propto N(0)$.
  • Band structure complexity in HEAs due to multiple atomic potentials.

  1. Library Design: Select 6–10 constituent elements (e.g., Ti, V, Cr, Nb, Ta, Mo)
  2. Deposition: Co-sputtering with moving shutter for gradient films
  3. Characterization: Resistance vs. temperature (4-probe) automated mapping
  4. ML Modeling: Regression of $T_c$ vs. features (atomic size mismatch, VEC, entropy)
  5. Validation: Synthesize bulk ingots of top candidates and measure detailed superconducting properties

functions6. Theoretical Modeling

  • First-Principles Calculations: DFT for electronic DOS and phonon spectra
  • Cluster Expansion: Predict enthalpy landscapes across composition space
  • High-Throughput Tools: AFLOW, Materials Project APIs for property querying

model_training7. Data-Driven Prediction

  • Feature Engineering: Combine composition descriptors, processing parameters
  • Algorithms: Random forests, gradient boosting, Gaussian process regression
  • Validation Metrics: RMSE of $T_c$, cross-validation score, discovery rate

biotech8. Experimental Validation

  • Bulk Synthesis: Arc melting, homogenization annealing
  • Characterization: SQUID magnetometry, four-point resistance, critical current density $J_c$
  • Phase Analysis: XRD for phase purity and lattice parameter trends

history_edu9. Case Studies

  • TaNbHfZrTi HEA: $T_c \approx 7.3\,\mathrm{K}$
  • MoNbReRuW HEA: predicted $T_c$ > 9 K (yet to be validated)

rocket_launch10. Future Prospects

  • Quantum Criticality: Tuning composition near electronic instabilities
  • Low-Dimensional HEAs: Thin films and heterostructures for interface superconductivity
  • Integrated Arrays: Patterned HEA superconducting circuits for quantum devices
