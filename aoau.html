All-Optical Arithmetic Units

Light-Only Operations via Interference Logic

highlight1. Introduction

All-optical arithmetic units perform mathematical operations—such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and logical evaluations—using solely light beams and passive/active photonic components. By encoding bits or analog values in optical amplitude, phase, or polarization, and using interference and nonlinear effects, complex arithmetic can be executed at the speed of light with minimal electronic conversion.

Key Advantages:

  • Ultra-High Speed: Terahertz bandwidth operations
  • Parallelism: Wavelength-division and spatial multiplexing enable massive concurrency
  • Low Latency: No electro-optic conversion delays
  • Integration: Compatible with silicon photonics for chip-scale systems

Constructive/Destructive Interference realizes logical operations:

  • XOR via balanced MZI with π phase difference
  • AND via nonlinear thresholding

3.2 Nonlinear Optical Effects

  • Kerr Effect: Intensity-dependent refractive index for threshold gating
  • Two-Photon Absorption: Implements optical AND by requiring two coincident photons

4.2 Full-Adder Composition

Cascade two half-adders and an OR gate (or add interference stage) to combine carries:

$$ Sum = A \oplus B \oplus C_{in}, \quad Carry = (A B) + (C_{in}(A \oplus B)) $$

tune5. Analog Arithmetic via Interference

5.1 Addition/Subtraction

Superpose multiple coherent beams; amplitude encodes analog values. Use interferometric splitters + combiners to sum optical fields:

$$ E_{out} = \sum_i E_i e^{i\phi_i} $$

Phase control allows subtraction by introducing π phase shifts.

5.2 Multiplication

  • Scalar Multiplication: Attenuation by variable optical attenuator (VOA)
  • Analog Multiplication: Four-wave mixing in nonlinear fiber: $E_{out} \propto E_1 E_2$

memory6. Photonic Integrated Circuit (PIC) Implementation

  • Platforms: Silicon-on-insulator, silicon nitride, InP
  • Components: MZIs, ring resonators, phase shifters (thermo-optic/electro-optic), nonlinear waveguides
  • Control: Microheaters or p-n junctions for dynamic tuning

report_problem8. Challenges & Future Work

  • Nonlinearity Efficiency: Enhance Kerr/nonlinear coefficients for compact devices
  • Stability: Mitigate phase drift via feedback control
  • Integration Density: Minimize crosstalk in dense PICs
  • Hybridization: Co-integration with quantum photonic elements for probabilistic operations
